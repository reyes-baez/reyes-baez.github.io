About and research interests

I am passionate about research and development in high-tech systems problems, where my education and experience allow me to propose innovative and practical solutions, as well as in related engineering science education. My background in mechatronics gives me the opportunity of interacting with interdisciplinary teams. I have scientific and engineering experience in systems and control for robotic systems, high-precision mechatronic systems, and wind turbine control. From an organizational perspective, I am adaptable and can work in teams or be self-directed, empathetic, and not afraid to ask questions.

From a scientific point of view, my research interests are the theory, engineering, and technology of nonlinear systems and control design, energy-based physical modeling for control, and data & learning-based control. Specifically:

  • Lyapunov, incremental and passivity techniques for control of nonlinear control systems

  • Lagrangian and port-Hamiltonian methods for modeling and control of physical and networked control systems

  • Data and learning-based control: Neural control, neural contraction metrics

  • Robotics and mechatronics: dynamics and control of rigid and soft robots, and of high-precision mechatronic systems,

  • Applications: opto-mechatronics, biomedical engineering, wind energy systems, smart industry
